How we score it · the rules
The goal is 98%.
Out of every 100 jobs we ship in a month, 98 should leave on or before the date we promised.
On or before the date counts.
Ship early, it counts. Ship on the day, it counts. Ship after the date, it’s late.
Pickup jobs count when they’re boxed and ready.
Most of our jobs are will call — the customer comes to us. Those count on the day they’re boxed and ready for pickup, because the wait after that belongs to the customer. Jobs we ship by carrier count on the day they leave the dock.
The score is simple division.
Jobs that made their date ÷ every job done that month — boxed for pickup, shipped for carrier. Nothing weighted, nothing hidden.
MRB lets you reschedule — it doesn’t skip the job.
A job held in MRB for at least a day can have its due date moved out by the days it was held, even past the original date. But you have to change the date. Reschedule it and ship by the new date, it’s on time. Leave the date and it’s late, even with MRB showing. A hold that clears the same day earns no reschedule — there’s no time to move. Managing the schedule is the job.
